## Lesson 6.1 — Fixture Standards and Water Efficiency

All plumbing fixtures installed in the United States must be listed to ANSI/ASME or other applicable standards and must meet **federal water-efficiency requirements** established under the Energy Policy Act (EPAct 1992) and updated by EPA WaterSense.

**Maximum flow rates (federal minimums / WaterSense targets):**

| Fixture | Federal Max | WaterSense Target |
|---|---|---|
| Toilet (flush) | 1.6 gpf | 1.28 gpf |
| Urinal | 1.0 gpf | 0.5 gpf |
| Lavatory faucet | 2.2 gpm @ 60 psi | 1.5 gpm |
| Kitchen faucet | 2.2 gpm @ 60 psi | 1.8 gpm |
| Showerhead | 2.5 gpm @ 80 psi | 2.0 gpm |

Many states and local jurisdictions have adopted stricter limits (e.g., California Title 20/24).

## Lesson 6.2 — Water Closets (Toilets)

### Types of Water Closets

| Type | Description | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Floor-mount (close-coupled) | Tank sits directly on the bowl; most common residential type | Residential |
| Floor-mount (one-piece) | Integrated tank and bowl, lower profile | Residential, premium |
| Wall-hung | Bowl is wall-mounted; tank is concealed in wall carrier system | Commercial, ADA renovations |
| Flushometer (pressure-assisted) | Uses supply pressure to flush; no gravity tank | Commercial |

### Rough-In Dimensions

**Standard floor-mount toilet rough-in:**
- **Flange center to finished back wall:** 12" (standard); 10" and 14" models available for existing roughing
- **Flange center to side wall / obstruction:** 15" minimum (18" ADA preferred)
- **Supply rough-in:** Cold water only; 6"–8" above finish floor, 6" to the left of flange center

**Diagram placeholder:** `[FIGURE 6-1: Toilet rough-in dimensions — 3-D floor plan and elevation with dimension annotations]`

### Closet Flange Installation

1. Install the DWV rough-in with the closet bend (4" × 3" reducer fitting in most cases) terminating below the finish floor.
2. Install the **closet flange** on top of the closet bend. The flange ring should rest on — or be caulked to — the finish floor surface (not recessed below it; per IPC 405.3.1, the flange top must be at the finished floor level or above for a wax ring to seal properly).
3. Secure the flange with fasteners into the subfloor.

### Water Closet Installation

1. Set **closet bolts (Johnny bolts)** in the flange slots, positioned with the long thread facing up.
2. Place a new **wax ring** on the flange horn or on the toilet horn (either method works; do not use both).
3. Lower the toilet bowl over the bolts — align with the flange — and press down firmly with full body weight to compress the wax ring. Do not rock or reposition after contact.
4. Install washers and nuts; tighten alternately — do not overtighten (crack the toilet base).
5. Connect the supply line to the ballcock/fill valve (hand-tight plus 1/4 turn with pliers).
6. Flush and inspect for leaks at the base and supply connection.

> ⚠ A wax ring is a one-time-use item. If the toilet is removed for any reason, install a new wax ring before reinstalling.

## Lesson 6.3 — Lavatories (Bathroom Sinks)

### Lavatory Types

| Type | Mounting Method | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Drop-in (self-rimming) | Drops into a countertop cutout; clips hold from below | Most common |
| Undermount | Mounted from below the countertop | Requires solid-surface or stone countertop |
| Pedestal | Free-standing; legs conceal the supply and drain connections | Space-saving, no countertop |
| Wall-hung | Mounted to wall bracket; fully ADA compliant | ADA, commercial |
| Vessel | Sits on top of the countertop | Decorative |

### Lavatory Rough-In

- **Drain centerline:** 17"–21" above finish floor (ADA: maximum 17" for knee clearance under wall-hung lavatory)
- **Hot supply:** Left side, 21" above finish floor, 2" left of drain centerline (standard 4" faucet hole spacing)
- **Cold supply:** Right side, 21" above finish floor, 2" right of drain centerline

### P-Trap and Drain Assembly

1. Connect the drain body (pop-up drain or grid drain) to the lavatory tailpiece.
2. Connect the P-trap's inlet to the tailpiece.
3. Connect the trap arm to the drain stub-out in the wall.
4. Ensure the trap arm slopes toward the wall (no back-slope) and does not exceed maximum trap arm length.

## Lesson 6.4 — Kitchen Sinks

Kitchen sinks present additional connections not found in bathrooms:
- **Garbage disposer** (food waste disposer) — connects to a dedicated disposer drain outlet in the sink basin; requires a 1-1/2" drain connection
- **Dishwasher drain** — 7/8" hose connects to the high-loop or air gap, then to the disposer inlet or sink drain tee
- **Dishwasher supply** — 3/8" compression or braided supply, hot water only
- **Water filtration / RO (reverse osmosis)** — additional supply stop and drain saddle

**Air gap requirement:** Many jurisdictions require a **deck-mounted air gap** (or a high drain loop) for the dishwasher drain to prevent back-siphonage of waste into the dishwasher.

## Lesson 6.5 — Bathtubs and Showers

### Bathtub Rough-In

- **Drain location:** Typically 15" from the head wall; check manufacturer for exact position
- **Supply:** Hot left, cold right; valve centered at 28"–30" AFF (above finish floor) — verify with manufacturer
- **Overflow:** 14"–20" above the drain centerline (tub-specific)
- **Shower valve height:** 36"–48" AFF for combination tub/shower; 48"–54" for stand-alone shower

**Tub drain assembly:** The tub waste-and-overflow fitting includes:
- **Outlet strainer / stopper** in the drain hole at the tub floor
- **Overflow plate** on the overflow hole above the waterline
- **Drain arm** connecting both through the wall to the trap

### Shower Pan / Pre-Slope

For tile shower floors, the installer applies a **pre-slope** (1/4" per foot minimum from all directions toward the drain) under the liner, and the liner itself must slope to drain holes in the shower drain body. The drain body has two-piece construction: the lower section bonds to the liner; the upper section screws into the lower and is adjusted to the finish-floor height.

**Flood test:** Before the liner is covered, fill the pan with water to the top of the liner for a minimum of 24 hours and verify no leakage occurs. This is typically a required inspection in most jurisdictions.

### Shower Valves and Scald Protection

ASSE 1016 (thermostatic mixing valve) or ASSE 1069 (pressure-balancing valve) are required for showers and tub/showers (IPC 424.3). The valve limits the outlet temperature to a maximum of **120 °F** to prevent scalding, particularly protecting children and the elderly.

- **Pressure-balancing valve (ASSE 1016 Type P):** Maintains a fixed hot/cold ratio when pressure changes occur; simple and inexpensive; cannot compensate for supply temperature fluctuations
- **Thermostatic mixing valve (ASSE 1016 Type T or T/P):** Maintains a preset temperature regardless of supply pressure or temperature variations; preferred in commercial and institutional settings

## Lesson 6.6 — Water Heater Installation

### Gas Water Heater Installation Sequence

1. **Location:** Must be in an accessible location. Gas appliances in sleeping rooms, bathrooms, or garages below floor level require a listed sealed-combustion unit.
2. **Gas supply:** Size per NFPA 54 / IFGC. Install shutoff valve (1/4-turn ball valve preferred) within 6 feet of the appliance.
3. **Seismic strapping:** Required in seismic zones (ASCE 7 areas); strap the upper 1/3 and lower 1/3 of the heater to the wall with 16-gauge steel straps and lag bolts into studs.
4. **Cold supply and hot outlet:** Install unions on both connections for serviceability; install isolation valves.
5. **T&P valve:** Install a listed T&P valve (ANSI Z21.22) in the designated opening or top of the tank. Pipe the discharge with full-port, non-restricted pipe to within 6" of the floor, drain pan, or exterior.
6. **Thermal expansion tank:** Install on the cold supply line if system is closed (see Module 4, Lesson 4.5).
7. **Venting:** Natural-draft gas heaters vent via a B-vent (double-wall) flue. The vent connector must pitch upward at minimum 1/4" per foot and connect to the vent above appliance level.
8. **Combustion air:** The mechanical room must provide adequate combustion air — either from infiltration (rooms ≥ 50 cubic feet per 1,000 BTU/hr) or through dedicated combustion air openings (two openings per NFPA 54).

**Diagram placeholder:** `[FIGURE 6-4: Gas water heater installation diagram — labeled 3-D showing gas line, T&P, expansion tank, seismic straps, and B-vent]`

### Electric Water Heater

Similar to gas except:
- No gas line, combustion air, or flue required
- Requires a dedicated 240V circuit (typically 30A double-pole breaker)
- Elements accessible from the side for replacement
- Still requires T&P valve, thermal expansion tank (in closed systems), and isolation valves

## Lesson 6.7 — Clothes Washers and Dishwashers

### Clothes Washer

**Connections required:**
- Hot and cold supply with individual shut-off valves (hose-bib type, 3/4" male hose thread)
- Floor drain or standpipe receptor (2" minimum standpipe, 18"–30" height above trap weir per IPC 802.4)
- The standpipe must be vented and the trap within the required distance

**Flood protection:** Consider an automatic flood-stop valve (Floodstop, Watts FloodSafe) on the supply hoses; standard washing machine hoses are a top cause of residential water damage.

### Dishwasher

**Connections required:**
- Hot water supply: 3/8" compression angle stop under the sink
- Drain: 7/8" hose to air gap or high-loop, then to disposal or sink drain
- Power: 120V dedicated circuit (hardwired in many models; cord-and-plug in others)

## Module 6 Summary

- Fixtures must meet listed standards and federal water-efficiency limits
- Toilet rough-in is typically 12" from flange center to finished back wall
- Wax rings are single-use; always replace when removing a toilet
- Shower and tub/shower valves must include scald protection per ASSE 1016/1069
- Water heater installation requires proper venting, combustion air, T&P relief, thermal expansion, and seismic strapping in applicable zones
